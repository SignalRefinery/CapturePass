# CapturePass Brand Specification

**Status:** Internal reference for the CapturePass rebrand  
**Brand:** CapturePass by HandshakeIQ

## Brand Names

- **Product Name:** CapturePass
- **Company Name:** HandshakeIQ
- **Full Brand:** CapturePass by HandshakeIQ
- **Never use:** CP
- **Legacy brand handling:** Treat TapTagg as a dead brand. Do not surface it to new users in public-facing brand language.

## Tagline

- **Primary Tagline:** Turn Every Handshake Into a Prospect.

## Positioning

- **Core Positioning Statement:** CapturePass helps professionals and teams capture contacts, share information instantly, and turn every handshake into a trackable business opportunity.

## Category Hierarchy

- **Primary Product Category:** Contact Capture Platform
- **SEO Category:** Digital Business Cards
- **Company Category:** Relationship Intelligence

### Category Rules

- CapturePass is not just a digital business card app.
- Digital business cards are an SEO acquisition category, not the primary product category.
- The main product category is contact capture.
- The broader company category is relationship intelligence.

## Brand Palette

- **Primary Blue:** `#0B5FFF`
- **Deep Blue:** `#0F4C81`
- **Insight Gold:** `#F5B301`
- **Success Green:** `#16A34A`
- **Charcoal:** `#111827`
- **Background:** `#FAFBFC`
- **White:** `#FFFFFF`

## Strategic Rules

- CapturePass is the public brand.
- HandshakeIQ is the company name behind CapturePass.
- TapTagg should not be introduced to new users as part of the public brand story.
- Use CapturePass consistently in product naming, marketing naming, and customer-facing references once rebrand work begins.
- Keep the category story centered on contact capture first, digital business cards second, and relationship intelligence as the broader company frame.
- Preserve brand consistency by avoiding abbreviations and legacy naming shortcuts.

## How to Use This Document During the Rebrand

Use this file as the source of truth before changing any customer-facing copy, metadata, tokens, or product labels.

- Check the product name and company name here before editing routes, headers, emails, or SEO copy.
- Use the category hierarchy to decide whether a page should lead with contact capture, digital business cards, or relationship intelligence.
- Use the palette values here when updating shared tokens or brand styling later in the rebrand.
- Follow the strategic rules to keep public messaging consistent and to avoid exposing TapTagg history to new users.
- Treat this document as the planning reference for later implementation phases, not as a behavior change.
